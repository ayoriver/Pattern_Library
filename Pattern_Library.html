Pattern Library

Our pattern library consists of frequently used page types with appropriate component placement and usage in an effort to best solve the members problem. It will go over the various different page types used, the level of navigation for each page, and the placement, order and usage of components. A pattern is always paired with the problem it solves and the situation it appears in. A solution applied in a context it wasnâ€™t designed for is not a pattern. A pattern must be consistently used throughout the site for the corresponding problem it is solving.

Purpose:

The purpose of a pattern is to:

  • Design the best possible member experience
  • Simplify the building of the UI
  • Create consistency accross the site

Page Layouts:

Page layouts provide patterns of common ways to arrange components and content on the screen.

Basic Page Layout:

The Basic page layout, known in Sitecore as the Single column page, is the entire user interface (UI) of a page. The Navigation menus and footer are global components that should always be active on the page. The body section are where components are placed to build out the page. In a single column page, the sections will have a uniform vertical gap of [40px]; no margin and padding will be necessary for each section.

Overview/Routing Pages:

Routing Pages serve as an overview of different products and services within a Business. They reside in a secondary or tertiary level within a product or service. And give a brief description, or list of details to inform the user to aid them in making their decision. In addition, they lead the user to more specific and detailed pages residing in a tertiary or quaternary level of page navigation.

Resource Pages:

Resource pages reside at a secondary level within a Business or Service. It consists of articles, widgets, checklists, links, and documents relevant to the Business or Service.

Users can utilize Resource pages to find additional information about a Product or Service either within SSFCU.org or through third-party and vendor sites. In addition, users can utilize the tools and widgets within the Resource page to aid in making decisions.

Contact & Directory Pages:

Contact and Directory pages reside on sub-primary navigation levels within a Business or Service.

They include accordions with bios on a particular Business or Service's team members, along with relevant contact info so that a member can get in contact with them.

They often will include a CTA to lead the member to a contact form.
